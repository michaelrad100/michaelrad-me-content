Recently a friend / VC posed an interesting question:

> Given that you're in consumer, I'll plant this seed: have you ever thought about starting a global AI Holdco in consumer products/services/experiences, and if so, how would you allocate $10M?

I sat with it for a day. Over the past few years I've been thinking about and witnessing the massive potential that's been created for consumer brands with AI. In the beginning of May, I turned my attention Holstee, after stepping back for a year, and in 3 short weeks, I've done more than I would have in 3 months at a fraction of the cost. So this question was timely for me, though I hadn't been thinking about it through this 10,000 ft holding company lens, though it didn't take long for me to see how quickly the benefits might stack.

Here's roughly what I sent back, lightly expanded after another week of sitting with it.

If I had $10M to allocate, my inclination would be a concentrated portfolio. I'd pick five to seven bets, weighted toward businesses where taste and brand are the moat and AI is operational leverage underneath.

The thesis underneath is that AI compresses the operational middle of a consumer business the way it already does in services. Sourcing, support, copy, logistics coordination, basic merchandising. The parts that used to need a team of competent operators are getting absorbed into a thinner layer of tooling. I'm watching it happen at [Holstee](https://www.holstee.com/) in small ways every week.

What that leaves is the part that was the real moat anyway. Taste built over years. A specific voice. A community that actually trusts the operator behind it. AI can't shortcut into any of that.

So the money would go toward founder-led brands where the person at the top has unusual taste and is willing to run lean. Less interest in AI-first consumer products that are mostly wrappers and lookalike copy. More interest in businesses that would have been excellent in 2010, run by someone willing to use 2026's leverage to do it with a fraction of the headcount.

These are first-pass thoughts. I'm continuing to sit with them and curious where I land in a few months. If you have a different cut, I'd love to hear it.
